Malware

Short for "malicious software," this includes viruses, worms, trojans, and spyware designed to disrupt systems or steal sensitive data [72][77]. A major concern is Ransomware, which encrypts files and demands a payment for their release [72].

Social Engineering

This attack manipulates human psychology to trick individuals into divulging confidential information. Common forms include Phishing (deceptive emails), Vishing (voice calls), and Smishing (SMS/text messages) [72][77].

DDoS Attacks

A Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) attack overwhelms a server or network with a flood of internet traffic, making the service unavailable to legitimate users. These attacks are often used for disruption or as a cover for other malicious activities [72][77].

AI-Powered Attacks

Cybercriminals are now using Artificial Intelligence to create more sophisticated attacks. This includes creating highly convincing Deepfakes for misinformation, or developing malware that can adapt in real-time to avoid detection [71][72].

Man-in-the-Middle

In a MitM attack, an attacker secretly intercepts and alters communications between two parties. This can be used to steal login credentials, financial information, or spy on conversations over unsecured networks like public Wi-Fi [33][72].
